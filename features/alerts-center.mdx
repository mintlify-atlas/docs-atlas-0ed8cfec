## Overview

The **Alerts Center** (Centro de Alertas y Oportunidades) is a proactive monitoring hub that automatically detects performance issues, risk situations, and growth opportunities across all business dimensions. Instead of discovering problems after they impact results, the system provides early warnings with context and recommended next steps.

## Alert Severity Levels

### 🔴 Critical (Alta)
Issues requiring immediate attention that significantly impact business results.

**Examples:**
- Seller with compliance less than 70% (ALT001)
- Margin below minimum threshold (ALT005)

**Expected response time:** Within 24 hours

### 🟡 Medium (Media)
Important issues that should be addressed soon to prevent escalation.

**Examples:**
- Customer without purchase in last 30 days (ALT002)
- Product with sales drop greater than 30% MoM (ALT003)
- Customer coverage less than 60% (ALT006)

**Expected response time:** Within 1 week

### 🔵 Low (Baja)
Informational items to monitor but not urgent.

**Examples:**
- Minor performance variations
- Seasonal pattern notifications

**Expected response time:** As time permits

### 🟢 Opportunity (Info)
Positive signals indicating growth potential or success.

**Examples:**
- High-frequency customer with low ticket (ALT007)
- Fast-growing products
- New customer acquisition

**Expected response time:** Plan strategic actions

<Info>
The severity level determines the alert's visual treatment in the interface and its priority in the list.
</Info>

## Implementation Details

The Alerts Center is implemented in `visor_kpi/pages/5_alertas.py`.

### Data Loading and Alert Generation

```python
with st.spinner("Evaluando alertas..."):
    data    = load_data()
    df      = get_filtered_data(fecha_desde, fecha_hasta, 
                                vendedores_sel, zonas_sel, canales_sel)
    df_ant  = get_filtered_data_periodo_anterior(fecha_desde, fecha_hasta, 
                                                  vendedores_sel, zonas_sel, canales_sel)
    cli_df  = data["clientes"]
    prod_df = data["productos"]
    obj_df  = data["objetivos"]
    alertas = get_alertas_activas(df, df_ant, cli_df, prod_df, obj_df, fecha_hasta)
```

The `get_alertas_activas()` function evaluates all alert rules and returns a DataFrame with active alerts.

```python
if "alertas_revisadas" not in st.session_state:
```

The application tracks which alerts have been marked as reviewed during the current session.

### Alert Filtering

Three-dimensional filtering for alert management:

```python
with col_f1:
    sev_filter = st.multiselect(
        "Severidad",
        ["alta", "media", "baja", "info"],
        default=[],
        format_func=lambda x: {"alta": "🔴 Crítica", "media": "🟡 Media",
                                "baja": "🔵 Baja", "info": "🟢 Oportunidad"}.get(x, x),
        key="_sev_filter",
    )
with col_f2:
    tipo_filter = st.multiselect(
        "Tipo de entidad",
        alertas["entidad_tipo"].unique().tolist() if not alertas.empty else [],
        default=[],
        key="_tipo_filter",
    )
with col_f3:
    mostrar_revisadas = st.checkbox("Ocultar revisadas", value=True, key="_hide_rev")

# Apply filters
alertas_vis = alertas.copy()
if sev_filter:
    alertas_vis = alertas_vis[alertas_vis["severidad"].isin(sev_filter)]
if tipo_filter:
    alertas_vis = alertas_vis[alertas_vis["entidad_tipo"].isin(tipo_filter)]
if mostrar_revisadas:
    alertas_vis = alertas_vis[
        ~alertas_vis.apply(lambda r: f"{r['id_alerta']}_{r['entidad_id']}", axis=1)
        .isin(st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"])
    ]
```

**Filter options:**
- **Severidad**: Filter by critical, medium, low, or opportunity
- **Tipo de entidad**: Filter by seller, customer, or product
- **Ocultar revisadas**: Hide alerts already marked as reviewed

### Review Action

Each unreviewed alert has an action button:

```python
if not ya_revisada:
    col_btn1, col_btn2, _ = st.columns([1, 1, 4])
    with col_btn1:
        if st.button("✅ Marcar revisada", key=f"_rev_{alerta_key}", 
                     help="Marcar como revisada en esta sesión"):
            st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"].add(alerta_key)
            st.rerun()
```

Marking an alert as reviewed:
1. Adds it to the session state set
2. Changes its visual appearance (faded opacity)
3. Can be hidden with the "Ocultar revisadas" filter
4. Tracked until session ends or history is cleared

<Note>
Reviewed status is session-based and resets when you reload the application. For production use, consider persisting review status to a database.
</Note>

## Alert Rules (ALT001-ALT007)

The system includes 7 pre-configured business rules:

### ALT001: Seller with Low Compliance
**Severity:** 🔴 Critical  
**Trigger:** Seller compliance less than 70%  
**Action:** Review with seller, identify obstacles, provide coaching

### ALT002: Customer Without Recent Purchase
**Severity:** 🟠 High  
**Trigger:** Customer with no purchase in 30+ days  
**Action:** Schedule visit, identify issues, offer promotions

### ALT003: Product with Significant Sales Drop
**Severity:** 🟠 High  
**Trigger:** Product sales decreased greater than 30% MoM  
**Action:** Investigate cause, adjust pricing, review competition

### ALT004: Excessive Portfolio Concentration
**Severity:** 🟡 Medium  
**Trigger:** Gini index greater than 0.6 (high dependency on few customers)  
**Action:** Diversify customer base, develop category B customers

### ALT005: Margin Below Threshold
**Severity:** 🔴 Critical  
**Trigger:** Gross margin below configured minimum  
**Action:** Review pricing strategy, reduce discounts, optimize costs

### ALT006: Low Customer Coverage
**Severity:** 🟠 High  
**Trigger:** Coverage less than 60% of assigned portfolio  
**Action:** Increase visit frequency, reassign territories, review capacity

### ALT007: Upsell Opportunity
**Severity:** 🟢 Opportunity  
**Trigger:** High-frequency customer with low average ticket  
**Action:** Promote higher-value products, bundle offerings, increase order size

<Info>
Alert rules are defined in `src/alerts_engine.py` and can be customized to match your business thresholds and priorities.
</Info>

## Customizing Alert Rules

Alert thresholds are configurable in `config.py`:

```python
config.py
# Alert thresholds
UMBRAL_CUMPLIMIENTO_CRITICO = 0.70  # ALT001 threshold
UMBRAL_DIAS_SIN_COMPRA = 30         # ALT002 threshold
UMBRAL_CAIDA_VENTAS = 0.30          # ALT003 threshold (30%)
UMBRAL_GINI_ALTO = 0.60             # ALT004 threshold
UMBRAL_COBERTURA_BAJA = 0.60        # ALT006 threshold
```

Adjust these values to match your business requirements and risk tolerance.
